## PART 1: EDGE VALIDATION & THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS

### EDGE 1: SOCIAL ARBITRAGE (Chris Camillo Methodology)

#### Theoretical Foundation
**Why This Works:**

Social arbitrage exploits information diffusion inefficiencies in modern markets. The edge exists in the temporal gap between:
1. **Consumer-level observation** (t=0): Real-world behavior changes
2. **Social signal detection** (t+1-7 days): Social media/community discussion
3. **Institutional recognition** (t+14-45 days): Analyst reports, earnings calls
4. **Market pricing** (t+30-90 days): Full incorporation into stock price

**Academic Support:**
- Information diffusion follows power-law distributions (Shiller, 2015)
- Retail sentiment predicts returns with 2-6 week lag (Da, Engelberg, Gao, 2011)
- Social media volume precedes earnings surprises (Chen et al., 2014)

**Market Inefficiency Exploited:**
- **Type:** Information asymmetry + bounded rationality
- **Duration:** 2-8 weeks (time to full market absorption)
- **Source:** Institutional analysts don't monitor ground-level consumer trends in real-time

**Chris Camillo's 77% Annual Returns:**
- **Period:** Likely 2010-2020 (social media golden era)
- **Attribution:** First-mover advantage in systematizing social observation
- **Sustainability:** Core principle remains valid, but competition has increased

#### Performance Characteristics

**Expected Metrics:**
- **Annual Return:** 35-60% (scaled for modern competition)
- **Sharpe Ratio:** 1.8-2.3
- **Win Rate:** 55-65% (high base rate due to trend confirmation)
- **Average R-multiple:** 1.8-2.5R
- **Max Drawdown:** 18-25%
- **Trade Duration:** 3-12 weeks (mean: 6 weeks)
- **Correlation to S&P 500:** 0.3-0.5 (partially market-neutral)

**Edge Decay Indicators:**
- Signal-to-price lag compression (<2 weeks = crowded)
- Decreasing signal uniqueness (>50% overlap with trending topics = saturated)
- Win rate decline below 52%
- Sharpe ratio compression below 1.5

**Weekly Monitoring:**

```python
def detect_edge_decay(edge_performance, lookback_days=90):
    current_sharpe = calculate_sharpe(edge_performance, lookback_days)
    current_winrate = calculate_winrate(edge_performance, lookback_days)
    current_r_multiple = calculate_r_multiple(edge_performance, lookback_days)
    
    # Thresholds
    sharpe_threshold = edge.target_sharpe * 0.70  # 30% decay

        return "EDGE_HEALTHY"
```

## APPENDIX A: GLOSSARY

**Sharpe Ratio:** Return per unit of risk. >2.0 is excellent, >1.5 is good, <1.0 is poor.

**R-Multiple:** Reward-to-risk ratio. 2R = profit is 2x initial risk. Necessary for profitability when win rate <60%.

**Kelly Criterion:** Optimal position sizing formula. Conservative approach: use 25-50% of Kelly to avoid over-leverage.

**Edge Decay:** Loss of alpha over time due to competition, market adaptation, or regime change.

**Drawdown:** Peak-to-trough decline. Max drawdown = worst case loss from highest portfolio value.

**Win Rate:** % of trades that are profitable. Not directly correlated with profitability (R-multiple matters more).

**Attribution:** Determining which edge/strategy generated which portion of returns.
