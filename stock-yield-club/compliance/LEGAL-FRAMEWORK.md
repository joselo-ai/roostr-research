## Insurance & Legal Protection

### Errors & Omissions (E&O) Insurance
**What it covers:** Claims of negligence, mistakes, or omissions in education  
**Cost:** $2k-$5k/year  
**Recommended:** Yes (cheap peace of mind)

### LLC Structure
**Why:** Shields personal assets from business liabilities  
**Setup:** Delaware or Wyoming LLC ($500 one-time)

### Member Agreement (Ironclad)
**Arbitration clause:** Disputes go to arbitration (not court)  
**Liability waiver:** Members waive right to sue for trading losses  
**Jurisdiction:** Delaware law (business-friendly)

## If SEC Comes Knocking

### Scenario: SEC sends inquiry letter

**DO:**
1. Hire securities attorney immediately
2. Provide requested documents
3. Cooperate fully
4. Review all disclaimers (make sure they're everywhere)

**DON'T:**
1. Ignore it (makes it worse)
2. Delete records (obstruction of justice)
3. Argue "it's just education" without legal counsel
4. Continue operating if told to cease

**Prevention:**
- Annual compliance audit (attorney reviews content)
- Maintain clear records
- Update disclaimers regularly
- Train team on compliance

## When to Upgrade to RIA (Future Decision)

### Signs You've Outgrown Education-Only Model:
1. Members demanding personalized portfolio advice
2. Revenue >$10M (attracting SEC attention)
3. Want to offer managed accounts (higher fees)
4. Institutional clients want fiduciary duty

### Cost of RIA Registration:
- Filing fees: $5k-$10k
- Ongoing compliance: $50k-$200k/year
- Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) required
- Annual audits, Form ADV filings

**Decision:** Only upgrade if revenue justifies compliance costs. Most education businesses stay below RIA threshold indefinitely.
