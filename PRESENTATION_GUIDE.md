**Act 1 (Slides 1-6): Setup**
- Establish problem, opportunity, solution
- Build credibility (tech, traction)
- **Pace:** Moderate (clear, methodical)

## Interactive Moments

### When to Pause for Questions

**Slide 4 (Agent Architecture):**
- Pause after explaining all 3 agents
- Ask: "Any questions on the architecture?"
- Common question: "How do the agents communicate?"
- Answer: "Through a shared data layer. Scraper writes signals, Atlas reads and validates, Edge reads validated signals and executes."

**Slide 7 (The Edges):**
- Pause after explaining Dan's 1000x TAO trade
- Common reaction: "How do you know this is replicable?"
- Answer: "Pattern recognition. Dan accumulated early, before mainstream attention. We're training the system to detect similar accumulation patterns."

**Slide 9 (99% Margins):**
- Pause after showing bar chart (wow moment)
- Common question: "What are the actual costs?"
- Answer: "At $1M AUM, we spend about $10k/year on cloud infrastructure. That's $800/month. GPT-4/Claude API costs, data storage, compute for backtesting."

**Slide 15 (The Ask):**
- Pause after stating valuation
- Common question: "Why $10M valuation with minimal revenue?"
- Answer: "Software-like unit economics. Traditional funds are valued at 2-3x revenue because they're labor-intensive. We should be valued like a SaaS company: 10-20x revenue. Plus first-mover advantage and proprietary tech."

### How to Handle Interruptions

**If interrupted mid-slide:**
1. Finish your sentence (don't stop abruptly)
2. Acknowledge question: "Great question. Let me address that."
3. Answer briefly (30-60 seconds)
4. Check if satisfied: "Does that answer your question?"
5. Resume: "Where was I? Right, [pick up where you left off]."

**If question is about a later slide:**
- "That's a great question. I actually have a slide on that coming up. Can I address it in 2 minutes?"
- If they insist, answer briefly, then say "I'll expand on this on Slide [X]."

**If you don't know the answer:**
- Don't bullshit. Say: "I don't have the exact number off the top of my head, but I can follow up with you after the pitch."
- Or: "That's a great question. Let me dig into that and get back to you."

## Body Language & Delivery

### Voice

**Tone:**
- Confident, not arrogant
- Technical, not jargon-heavy
- Conversational, not scripted
- Slightly edgy, not corporate

**Volume:**
- Loud enough to fill room (test before presenting)
- Vary volume (louder for emphasis, quieter for intimacy)

**Pace:**
- Normal: 130-150 words per minute
- Slow down for key numbers ("99% margins", "$10M valuation")
- Speed up for lists (don't drag on bullets)

**Pauses:**
- Pause after key statements (let them land)
- Pause before transitions (signal slide change)
- Pause for questions (invite engagement)

### Gestures

**Use hands to:**
- Point to specific elements on slide ("Look at this bar chart")
- Emphasize numbers (count on fingers: "One: Technology. Two: Market. Three: First-mover.")
- Show scale (hands wide apart: "From $1M to $100M")

**Avoid:**
- Fidgeting (hands in pockets, playing with remote)
- Repetitive gestures (same hand wave 20 times)
- Blocking screen (stand to side, not in front of projector)

### Eye Contact

**Where to look:**
- Scan the room (don't fixate on one person)
- Make eye contact with decision-makers (partners, lead investors)
- Glance at screen briefly (confirm slide, then back to audience)

**Avoid:**
- Reading from slides (you should know the content)
- Staring at laptop (use presenter view, but look up)
- Looking at floor/ceiling (nervous tell)

### Stance

**Stand:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart (stable, confident)
- Weight balanced (not shifting side to side)
- Shoulders back, chest open (confident posture)

**Move:**
- Occasional steps (toward audience for emphasis, back for transitions)
- Avoid pacing (nervous energy)
- Stay in "sweet spot" (visible to everyone, not blocking screen)

## Q&A Preparation

### Common Questions & Answers

#### 1. "How is this different from a traditional quant fund with algos?"

**Answer:**
> "Traditional quant funds use statistical models trained on historical data. We're using LLMs for qualitative analysis: reading social sentiment, understanding narratives, detecting early signals. It's closer to how human analysts think, but at machine scale."

#### 2. "What if the LLM hallucinates or makes a wrong call?"

**Answer:**
> "That's why we have multi-source validation (Atlas agent). A signal needs to be corroborated across on-chain data, GitHub, social sentiment, etc. We also have human oversight (me) on final risk management and position sizing."

#### 3. "Why can't Citadel or Two Sigma just copy this?"

**Answer:**
> "They will, eventually. But they're slow. They have legacy infrastructure, compliance layers, 2,000+ employees. We're AI-native from day one. By the time they ship, we'll have 18-24 months of proprietary data and a validated track record."

#### 4. "What's your moat? Can't anyone build this with GPT-4?"

**Answer:**
> "The moat is the data pipelines and validated workflows. Yes, anyone can call the GPT-4 API. But we've built proprietary social signal extraction, multi-source corroboration algorithms, and backtested strategies. It's the system, not just the LLM."

#### 5. "Have you actually deployed capital? What are your live returns?"

**Answer:**
> "We're in Phase 1: deploying $100k over the next 90 days to prove returns. We've validated 4 strategies post-hoc (Camillo's +77%, Riz's $120k+, etc.), but we're not pitching backtests. We're pitching the infrastructure to scale this systematically."

#### 6. "What if there's a regulatory crackdown on AI trading?"

**Answer:**
> "We're structured as a traditional hedge fund with human oversight. I make final decisions on risk management. The AI agents are tools, not autonomous traders. This is no different legally than quant funds using algorithms."

#### 7. "What if LLM costs skyrocket?"

**Answer:**
> "Even if API costs 10x, we're still 90%+ margins. The unit economics work even at much higher LLM pricing. Plus, open-source models (Llama, Mistral) provide competitive pressure on pricing."

#### 8. "How do you handle conflicts of interest (e.g., pumping tokens you own)?"

**Answer:**
> "We're building in public (GitHub repo), so all signals and trades are transparent. We're also not a market maker or exchange. We're a fund that buys and holds. No incentive to pump and dump."

#### 9. "What's the exit strategy for investors?"

**Answer:**

## Remote Presenting (Zoom/Google Meet)

### Setup

**Video:**
- HD webcam (1080p minimum)
- Good lighting (ring light or natural light from window)
- Clean background (or virtual background with roostr branding)
- Camera at eye level (stack books under laptop if needed)

**Audio:**
- External mic (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB, or AirPods Pro)
- Quiet room (no background noise)
- Test audio before call (ask someone to confirm quality)

**Screen Share:**
- Share specific window (deck only, not entire screen)
- Close distractions (email, Slack, notifications)
- Use "Optimize for video clip" setting (smoother animations)

### Presenting on Zoom

**Before starting:**
1. Mute notifications (Do Not Disturb mode)
2. Close unnecessary apps (free up CPU for smooth screen share)
3. Load deck in full-screen presentation mode
4. Start screen share: Share â†’ Select deck window â†’ "Optimize for video clip"

**During presentation:**
- Look at camera (not screen) when talking (eye contact)
- Glance at screen occasionally (confirm slide, then back to camera)
- Use cursor to point to elements (circle/highlight on Zoom)
- Pause longer than in-person (internet lag, give them time to react)

**Energy:**
- 20% more energy than in-person (Zoom saps energy, compensate)
- Smile more (builds rapport through screen)
- Use hand gestures (even if they can't see your hands, it energizes your voice)

### Technical Troubleshooting

**If screen share freezes:**
- Stop share, restart share (have backup tab ready)
- Worst case: Send deck via Zoom chat, ask them to open on their end

**If audio cuts out:**
- Rejoin call (have link saved)
- Call in via phone (have dial-in number ready)

**If deck won't load:**
- Have PDF backup in multiple locations (Google Drive, email to self, USB)
- Worst case: Present from memory (you know the content)
